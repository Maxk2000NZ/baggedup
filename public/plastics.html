Disc Golf
Plastic Guide

Every plastic type from every major manufacturer — grip, durability, flight characteristics, and recommended uses. The complete reference.

⬜ Base Plastic

Entry Level

Cheapest option. Grippy in cold. Wears in quickly — often making overstable discs more understable over time. Great for learning new moulds.

🟠 Premium Plastic

Tournament Grade

More durable, consistent flight characteristics, great grip in all conditions. Standard for serious play. Holds its stability much longer than base.

🟣 Specialty Blends

Special Edition

Premium blends with unique feels, looks, or specific performance tuning. Glow plastics, metal flake, colour-shifting, extra-grippy formulas.

🦅
Innova
The world's largest disc golf manufacturer. Founded 1983.
Plastic Name Tier Durability Grip Wear-In Notes
DX Base
Fast (5–20 rounds) Most affordable Innova plastic. Excellent beginner grip, especially in cold. Becomes more understable as it wears. Ideal for learning mould.
Pro Base+
Medium (20–50 rounds) Slightly more durable than DX. Still has great grip. Less common now but still used in putters like the Pro Aviar.
Star Premium
Very slow (100+ rounds) Innova's flagship premium. Excellent durability, consistent flight, slightly rubber-like grip. One of the most popular tournament plastics globally.
Champion Premium
Extremely slow (200+ rounds) Harder, more rigid plastic. Near zero wear-in — flies exactly as rated for a very long time. Less grip than Star, especially in cold. Used by pros for discs they want to stay overstable.
Halo Star Specialty
Very slow Star plastic with striking Halo rim design. Two-tone look, premium feel, slightly stiffer than standard Star. Very popular collector/tournament series.
Glow Champion Glow
Extremely slow Champion plastic that glows in the dark after UV exposure. Practical for night rounds. Flies identical to standard Champion.
Blizzard Champion Specialty
Very slow Ultra-lightweight Champion plastic with microbubbles. Can float on water. Often 1–2g lighter than standard — changes flight characteristics (more distance for slower arms). Popular for beginners who can't throw standard Destroyers far enough.
🎯
Discraft
Michigan-made. Home of the Buzzz and the Z-line. Paul McBeth's brand.
Plastic NameTierDurabilityGripWear-InNotes
Pro-D / Putter Line Base
Fast Soft, grippy, affordable. Very popular for putting in cold weather. Wears in quickly on drives.
Z Line Premium
Very slow Discraft's primary premium line. Glossy finish, translucent, great flight consistency. Equivalent to Innova Champion in feel and durability. Very popular for Buzzz, Zone, and drivers.
ESP Premium
Very slow Premium blend with more flexibility than Z-line. Excellent grip. Popular for the Buzzz OS and Zone. Feels more "premium" in hand than Z to many players.
Titanium (Ti) Specialty
Extremely slow Discraft's most durable plastic. Hard, rigid, excellent for overstable discs you want to keep overstable long-term. Less popular due to low grip in cold.
Z Glo Glow
Very slow Z-line plastic that charges and glows under UV. Same flight properties as standard Z. Great for night events.
Cryztal Z Specialty
Very slow Crystal-clear Z plastic. Extremely translucent — you can see through it. Same durability as Z but more flexible with better cold-weather grip. Very eye-catching on the course.
⚡
Dynamic Discs
Part of the Latitude 64 / Westside Discs family (Trilogy brands).
Plastic NameTierDurabilityGripWear-InNotes
Classic Base
Fast Softest base-level plastic in the Trilogy lineup. Very grippy and flexible. Wears in quickly, good for putting practice.
Classic Blend Base+
Medium Slightly more durable than Classic. Retains the excellent grip. Popular for Judge putters used for driving.
Lucid Premium
Very slow DD's standard premium. Great consistency, translucent finish, popular with tournament players. Widely used for Felon, Defender, Sheriff.
Fuzion Premium
Very slow More flexible than Lucid. Better grip especially in cold or wet conditions. Preferred by many players for approach and putter discs.
Lucid-X Specialty
Extremely slow DD's most durable plastic. Very hard and rigid. Used for discs you want to keep highly overstable. Less grip than Lucid/Fuzion.
Fuzion-X Specialty
Extremely slow Fuzion-X combines the durability of Lucid-X with slightly more grip. Best of both worlds for players who want max durability but need cold-weather grip.
🔵
Discmania
Finnish-born brand. Founded with Innova partnerships. Now independent.
Plastic NameTierDurabilityGripWear-InNotes
D-Line Base
Fast Equivalent to DX. Affordable, grippy base plastic. Very popular for the P2 putter in putting-focused bags.
C-Line Premium
Extremely slow Very hard, rigid, max durability. Similar to Innova Champion. Great for overstable discs — they stay overstable for hundreds of rounds. Low grip in cold.
S-Line Premium
Very slow Discmania's equivalent of Innova Star. More flexible than C-line, better grip, slightly faster wear. Very popular choice for drivers and fairways.
Neo Specialty
Very slow Modern premium blend. Slightly translucent, excellent grip, very popular for the Magic putter. Great all-weather performance.
Exo Soft / Hard Specialty
Medium–slow Two variants — Soft for maximum grip and feel, Hard for more durability. Both are very popular for putting. One of the softest, grippiest putter plastics on the market.
🔴
MVP / Axiom Discs
Michigan-made. Famous for overmould rim technology and "gyro" physics.
Plastic NameTierDurabilityGripWear-InNotes
Neutron Premium
Very slow MVP's flagship premium. Excellent durability, consistent flight, available in many colours. Popular across their full lineup. The "standard" premium choice from MVP.
Proton Premium
Extremely slow Hardest MVP plastic. Maximum durability and stability retention. Great for overstable discs in wind conditions.
Plasma Specialty
Very slow Swirling, marbled colours — one of the most visually striking plastics in disc golf. Premium-grade durability and excellent grip. Each disc is a unique colour combination.
Electron Base
Medium Available in Soft and Medium variants. One of the grippiest putter plastics available. The Soft is particularly popular for wet-weather putting. More durable than typical base plastic.
🌍
Latitude 64
Swedish manufacturer. Part of Trilogy alongside Dynamic Discs and Westside.
Plastic NameTierDurabilityGripWear-InNotes
Zero Hard / Soft Base
Fast Excellent putter plastic. Soft version is ultra grippy — preferred in cold, wet weather. Hard has more structure for driving putters. Very popular for the Compass and Pure.
Gold Premium
Extremely slow Hard, durable, excellent for drivers. Very stable flight characteristics that hold for a long time. Equivalent to Innova Champion.
Opto Premium
Very slow More flexible than Gold with better grip. Lat 64's primary tournament plastic. Excellent all-condition performance. Popular for River, Culverin, Saint Pro.
Royal Specialty
Very slow Premium blend with metallic sheen. Great grip and excellent durability. Popular collector series. Flies identically to standard Opto.

💡 All disc brands in the BaggedUp app can be filtered by plastic type. When comparing discs, keep plastic in mind — the same mould in base plastic vs premium can fly noticeably differently, especially early in its life.
